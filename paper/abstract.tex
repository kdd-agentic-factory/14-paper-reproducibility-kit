\begin{abstract}
We present a KDD-governed agentic architecture for race engineering in the context
of elite motorcycle racing. The system integrates a multi-repository ecosystem of
fourteen specialised components --- from agent orchestration and tool gateways to
knowledge retrieval, observability, and CI/CD --- governed end-to-end by a
Knowledge-Driven Development methodology. A race AI copilot translates raw
telemetry into evidence-grounded decision support delivered through a Race Command
Center dashboard, while a Digital Twin simulation layer validates recommendations
before they reach the engineering team. We describe the architecture, the agentic
workflow model, the security and compliance controls, and an experimental evaluation
across nine controlled studies. The accompanying reproducibility kit packages all
synthetic datasets, experiment configurations, generated tables and figures,
enabling independent verification of reported results.

\textbf{Keywords:} agentic AI; race engineering; knowledge-driven development;
decision support; telemetry analysis; digital twin; reproducibility.
\end{abstract}
