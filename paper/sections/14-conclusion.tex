\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented a KDD-governed agentic architecture for race engineering that
addresses three open problems in the deployment of LLM-based systems in
high-stakes operational domains: uncontrolled generation, tool access security,
and reproducibility.

The architecture achieves:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $94\%$ traceability score under \kdd~governance (vs $41\%$ baseline),
  \item $99\%$ audit coverage through \mcp~gateway routing,
  \item $81\%$ recommendation groundedness with \rag/\cag~augmentation,
  \item $23\%$ unsafe recommendation blocking by the digital twin.
\end{itemize}

The Paper Reproducibility Kit consolidates the architecture, workflows, datasets,
metrics, experiments, security controls, deployment artefacts, and generated
results into a reproducible scientific package, enabling independent validation
of the proposed KDD-governed agentic race engineering ecosystem.

The architecture is designed for extension: each repository has a single declared
responsibility and communicates through standardised interfaces, allowing
components to be replaced or extended without affecting the governance model.
Future work will focus on real-time streaming integration, real-world dataset
validation, and extension to other high-stakes engineering domains.
